Research Works
  • Cyber Crime, Cyber Law and its Awareness [view report]

    Jony Ahmmed, Rukaiya Nur Raka, Masuma Khandakar

    Nowadays, cybercrime is one of the vital crimes done by computer specialist. Cybercrime is a common word that we always heard in this globalization era. Cyber security is to provide prevention against the cybercrime, while cybercrime is that group of activities made by the people by creating disturbance in network, stealing others important and private data, documents, hack bank details and accounts and transferring money to their own. Cyber law is any law that applies to the internet and internet-related technologies. Cyber law is one of the newest areas of the legal system. Cyber law provides legal protections to people using the internet. Over the past two decades, cybercrime has become an increasingly widely debated topic across many walks of life. This paper gives detailed information regarding cybercrime and cyber law. It includes types of cybercrime, need of cybercrime, issues in cybercrime, its advantages and disadvantages, history of cybercrime, different country cyber law and its awareness.

  • Performance Analysis of Different Sorting Algorithms [view report]

    Jony Ahmmed

    Sorting is the basic operation in most of the applications of computer science. Sorting means to arrange data in particular order inside computer. In this paper we have discussed performance of different sorting algorithms with their advantages and disadvantages. This paper also represents the application areas for different sorting algorithms. Main goal of this paper is to compare the performance of different sorting algorithms based on different parameters. Sorting is a huge demand research area in computer science and one of the most basic research fields in computer science. The sorting algorithms problem has attracted a great deal of study in computer science. The main aim of using sorting algorithms is to make the record easier to search, insert, and delete. We’re analyzing a total of five sorting algorithms: bubble sort, selecting sort, insertion sort, merge sort and quick sort, the time and space complexity were summarized. Moreover from the aspects of the input sequence, some results were obtained based on the experiments. So we analyzed that when the size of data is small, insertion sort or selection sort performs well and when the sequence is in the ordered form, insertion sort or bubble sort performs well. In this paper, we present a general result of the analysis of sorting algorithms and their properties. In this paper a comparison is made for different sorting algorithms.
